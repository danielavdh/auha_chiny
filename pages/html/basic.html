Basic Setup of an HTML Page:

1. The absolute minimum:

<html> Telling the browser: "Here comes an HTML document!"
    <head> The "head" is for content that won't be seen. Here you can add information about your document.
    </head> Closing tag: End of HEAD.
 
    <body> The "body" is for the content you want to show to your visitors. This could be text, images, sound, films, links etc.
    </body> Closing tag: End of BODY.
</html> Closing tag: End of HTML document.

2. Let's add something to the body!

Each tag tells the browser what is coming next. The closing tag tells the browser: Message over.


<body> Make sure you nest your tags, so you easily see what is inside what (4 spaces or 1 tab).
    <h1>Welcome! "H1" or "h1" is a title tag (header). There are 6 of them: h1-h6. H1 is the most important header in your document.
    </h1> H1 closing tag: End of H1. The browser now knows that "Welcome!" is meant to be shown as a title.
 
    <div> This is a "DIV" or "div" tag. There is nothing in it at the moment.
    </div> DIV closing tag: End of DIV.
</body> BODY closing tag: End of body.

3. Some things to put in the head:

In the head you can put information about your document (meta tags), you can add a title tag, and you can tell the browser where to find other files that are needed for your page, like stylesheets and scripts.


<head>
    <meta http-equiv="content-type" content="text/html; charset=utf-8" />
    <meta http-equiv="content-language" content="en" />
    <meta name="description" content="Hermione Lovelace's backstage tour of the web" />
    <meta name= "keywords" content="HTML, CSS, Python, Javascript" />
    <meta name="author" content="Hermione Lovelace" />
    <meta name="copyright" content="2017, Hermione Lovelace" />
    
    <title>Welcome!</title>
    
    <link rel="stylesheet" media="all" href="basic.css" />
</head>

In the head above we have 6 meta tags giving information about your document, 1 title tag with "Welcome!" as its content, and 1 link to a stylesheet.

Tags that NEVER have a content (like "LINK" or "META" or "IMG") don't need an opening AND a closing tag - though of course it is perfectly ok to write them. You can use a "shorthand" version: Instead of writing <img></img>, you can just write <img />.

Information that is given INSIDE the opening tags is called ATTRIBUTES of the tags. For example your "link" tag above to your stylesheet has 3 attributes: "rel", "media" and "href". We will talk about tag attributes very soon.
